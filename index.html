Rites of the Irreducible

Three movements through the aftermath of a single event that cannot be named directly. The first attempts to index it — the mind's instrument of control applied to what resists control. The second enters the body — words spoken and unretractable, words withheld and perhaps recoverable, all of it cast into water and watched. The third arrives at the question neither movement could answer: whether forgiveness is a process or a grace, and whether mercy can be made or only received.
